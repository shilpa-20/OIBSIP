Savitribai Phule

(3 Jan 1831- 10 Mar 1897)

INDIA'S FIRST WOMAN TEACHER

Savitribai Jyotirao Phule was born in Naigaon, British India, now in Satara districton 3 January 1831 and died in Pune,Maharashtra, British India on 10 March 1897.she was an Indian social reformer and poet. Along with her husband- Jyotirao Phule, she played an important role in improving women's rights in India during British rule.her to Jyotirao Phule educated her at home and trained her to become a teacher. Along with her husband, also a social reformer, she opened 18 schools for girls, going on to become India's first woman teacher and headmistress. Both her first and 18th school came up in Maharashtra's Pune. In her honour, the University of Pune was renamed Savitribai Phule University in 2014. Phule, along with her husband founded the first girls'school in Pune run by native Indians at Bhide Wada in 1848. She worked to abolish discrimination and unfair treatment of people based on caste and gender. She is regarded as an important figure of the social reform movement in Maharashtra.
